Odessa2022

by David Grigoryan

On the morning of February 24, as usual, I went for a walk to the sea, and on the way everything was as usual: a lot of people on the streets, in the park, open shops, ships on the horizon, etc.
As soon as I returned home, my flatmate, who had listened to Zelensky's speech before I went for a walk, came into my room and said that the war had begun.
I didn't want to, but I had to believe. Despite this, I went to my photo lab. The mood is a bit tense.
People are already visible with suitcases and cars full of belongings. The phone was torn from the question - “are you safe?” from foreign friends. Nevertheless, I finished printing and returned home a little confused. I didn’t think of anything else than to go for a walk again in the already noticeably empty Shevchenko Park. Thus ended my first day. The next day I barricaded the room: I sealed the windows, made them with plywood, hung blankets from the eaves and moved the mattress away from the window. I remembered my childhood and the first war that I found in Tbilisi. My baby bed was surrounded by chairs and pillows with blankets on them, all covered by closed shutters. Because of this shit, my family flew from Georgia to Odessa in 1993. Despite the fact that I was 6 years old, the memories of that time remained.
Now I'm already at a conscious age and history has repeated itself.
The most useful thing that I personally could do was shooting what was happening in Odessa. I was a fixer for foreign journalists - I helped them write articles, translate interviews, searched for hotels, drivers who would meet them at the border of Transnistria, and others who would be ready to take them to Nikolaev and Voznesensk. In short, I did everything so that they showed the truth to the whole world!
